Let \(G\) be a graph whose vertices are the integers. Assume that any two integers are connected by a finite path in \(G\). For two integers \(x\) and \(y\), we denote by \(d(x,y)\) the length of the shortest path from \(x\) to \(y\), where the length of a path is the number of edges in it. Assume that \(d(x,y)\mid x-y\) for all \(x,y\in\Z\) and define \(S(G) = \{d(x,y)\mid x,y\in\Z\}\). Find all possible sets \(S(G)\).
