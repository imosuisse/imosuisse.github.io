Let \(\Gamma_1\) and \(\Gamma_2\) be two circles intersecting at the points \(X\) and \(Y\). Let \(\ell\) be a line intersecting \(\Gamma_1\) at points \(A\) and \(C\), and intersecting \(\Gamma_2\) at points \(B\) and \(D\), such that \(A, B, C, D\) lie on \(\ell\) in this order. Let \(P\) be the intersection of \(XA\) and \(YB\), and \(Q\) be the intersection of \(XD\) and \(YC\).

Prove that \(PQ\) is parallel to \(\ell\).
