Let \(A=(a_1,a_2,\ldots,a_n)\) be a sequence of integers. The successor of \(A\) is the sequence \(A‘=(a’_1,a‘_2,\ldots,a’_n)\) with \[a'_k = |\{i<k \mid a_i<a_k\}|-|\{i>k \mid a_i>a_k\}|.\] Let \(A_0\) be a finite sequence of integers and for \(k \geq 0\) let \(A_{k+1}=A'_k\) be the successor of \(A_k\). Show that a natural number \(m\) exists with \(A_m=A_{m+1}\).
