Let \(n\) be a positive integer. Prove that there exists a finite sequence \(S\) consisting of only zeros and ones, satisfying the following property: For any positive integer \(d\geq 2\), when \(S\) is interpreted as a number in base \(d\), the resulting number is non-zero and divisible by \(n\). Remark: The sequence \(S = s_ks_{k-1}\cdots s_1s_0\) interpreted in base \(d\) is the number \(\sum_{i = 0}^k s_id^i\).
