Define the sequence \((a_n)_{n\geq 0}\) of integers by \(a_n:=2^n+2^{\floor{n/2}}\). Prove that there are infinitely many terms of the sequence which can be expressed as a sum of two or more distinct terms of the sequence, as well as infinitely many terms which cannot be expressed in such a way.
