Let \(n\) be a natural integer and \(G\) be the set of points \((x,y)\) in the plane such that \(x\) and \(y\) are integers with \(1\leq x,y\leq n\). A subset of \(G\) is called parallelogramfree if it does not contain four non-collinear points that are the vertices of a parallelogram. How many points at most can a parallelogramfree subset contain?
