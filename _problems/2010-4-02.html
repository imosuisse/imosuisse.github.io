Let \(AB\) be the diameter of the circle \(k\). Let \(t\) be the tangent to \(k\) at point \(B\) and let \(C,D\) be two points on \(t\) such that \(B\) lies between \(C\) and \(D\). The lines \(AC\) and \(AD\) intersect \(k\) again at the points \(E\) and \(F\). The lines \(DE\) and \(CF\) intersect \(k\) again at the points \(G\) and \(H\) respectively. Prove that the lines \(AG\) and \(AH\) have the same length
