Let \(ABC\) be a triangle, \(M\) the midpoint of \(BC\) and \(D\) a point on the line \(AB\) such that \(B\) lies between \(A\) and \(D\). Let \(E\) be a point such that \(E\) and \(B\) are on different sides with respect to the line \(CD\) and such that \(\angle EDC = \angle ACB\) and \(\angle DCE = \angle BAC\). Let \(F\) be the intersection point of \(CE\) with the parallel line to \(DE\) through \(A\). Let \(Z\) be the intersection point of \(AE\) and \(DF\). Prove that the lines \(AC\), \(BF\) and \(MZ\) intersect in a point.
