The points \(A\), \(M_1\), \(M_2\) and \(C\) lie on a line in this order. Let \(k_1\) be the circle with centre \(M_1\) through \(A\) and \(k_2\) the circle with centre \(M_2\) through \(C\). The two circles intersect at the points \(E\) and \(F\). A common tangent to \(k_1\) and \(k_2\) touches \(k_1\) in \(B\) and \(k_2\) in \(D\). Show that the lines \(AB\), \(CD\) and \(EF\) intersect at a point.
