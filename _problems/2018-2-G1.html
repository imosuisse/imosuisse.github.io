Let \(ABC\) be a triangle and let \(\gamma = \angle ACB\). Assume that \(\gamma /2 < \angle BAC\) and \(\gamma/2 < \angle CBA\) hold. Let \(D\) be the point on the side \(BC\) such that \(\angle BAD = \gamma/2\). Let \(E\) be the point on the side \(CA\) such that \(\angle EBA = \gamma/2\). Let furthermore \(F\) denote the intersection point of the angle bisector of \(\angle ACB\) with the side \(AB\). Prove that \(EF + FD = AB\).
