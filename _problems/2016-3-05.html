Let \(ABC\) be a triangle with \(\angle ACB = 90^\circ\) and let \(M\) be the midpoint of \(AB\). Let \(G\) be an arbitrary point on the segment \(MC\) and let \(P\) be a point on \(AG\) for which \(\angle CPA = \angle BAC\). Further, let \(Q\) be a point on \(BG\) for which \(\angle BQC = \angle CBA\). Show that the circumcircles of triangles \(AQG\) and \(BPG\) intersect on \(AB\).
