Let \(k\), \(n\) and \(r\) be positive integers with \(r < n\). Mickey has \(kn + r\) black socks and \(kn + r\) white socks. He wants to hang them on a straight clothesline in such a way that there are no \(2n\) consecutive socks of which \(n\) of them are black and \(n\) of them are white. Show that he can do this if and only if \(r \geq k\) and \(n > k + r\) holds.
