A positive integer \(x\) is called good if \(x\) is the product of an even number of (not necessarily different) prime numbers. Let \(a,b\) be positive integers and define \(m(x)=(x+a)(x+b)\).

  1. Prove that two different positive integers \(a,b\) exist such that \[m(1),m(2), \ldots, m(2010)\] are all good numbers.

  2. If \(m(x)\) is good for every positive integers \(x\), then \(a=b\).
