Let \(S\) be a nonempty set of positive integers. Prove that at least one of the following two assertions holds:

  1. There exist distinct finite nonempty subsets \(F\) and \(G\) of \(S\) such that \[\sum_{x\in F}\frac{1}{x}=\sum_{x\in G}\frac{1}{x}.\]

  2. There exists a positive rational number \(r<1\) such that, for all finite nonempty subsets \(F\) of \(S\), \[\sum_{x\in F} \frac{1}{x}\neq r.\]
