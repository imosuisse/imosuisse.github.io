Let \(n\) be a positive integer. A volleyball team consisting of \(n\) men and \(n\) women is preparing to play. Every player is assigned one of the positions \(1,2,\ldots,2n\) of which exactly the positions \(1\) and \(n+1\) lie outside the court. During the game, the players rotate in such a way that the player on position \(i\) switches to position \(i+1\) (respectively from position \(2n\) to \(1\)). In how many ways can the positions initially be assigned so that there are always at least \(n-1\) women on the court, no matter how many rotations have occured?

Remark: Two initial positions are different, if at least one player is assigned to two different positions.
