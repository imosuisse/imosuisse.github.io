Let \(a_1,a_2,\ldots, a_{2007}\) be a sequence that contains each of the numbers \(1,2,\ldots,2007\) exactly once. The following operation is now performed repeatedly: If the first sequence member is equal to \(n\), then the order of the first \(n\) sequence members is reversed. Show that the sequence begins with the number \(1\) after a finite number of such operations.
