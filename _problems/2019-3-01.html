Let \(A\) be a point and \(k\) a circle through \(A\). Let \(B\) and \(C\) be two other points on \(k\). Let now \(X\) be the intersection of the angle bisector of \(\angle ABC\) with \(k\). Further, let \(Y\) be the reflection of \(A\) in point \(X\), and \(D\) be the intersection of the line \(YC\) and \(k\). Show that the point \(D\) does not depend on the choice of the points \(B\) and \(C\) on the circle \(k\).
