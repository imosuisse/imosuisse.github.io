Two circles \(\omega_1\) and \(\omega_2\) touch tangentially at a point \(A\) and lie inside a circle \(\Omega\). Furthermore, \(\omega_1\) touches \(\Omega\) tangentially at a point \(B\) and \(\omega_2\) touches \(\Omega\) tangentially at a point \(C\). The line \(AC\) intersects \(\omega_1\) a second time at a point \(D\). Show that the triangle \(DBC\) is right-angled if \(A\), \(B\) and \(C\) are not collinear.
