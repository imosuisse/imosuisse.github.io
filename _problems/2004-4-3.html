--- year: 2004 round: 4 problem: 3 ---

Let \(ABC\) be an isosceles triangle with \(|AC|=|BC|\) with incircle \(I\). Let \(P\) be a point on the circumcircle of the triangle \(AIB\) which lies within the triangle \(ABC\). The lines through \(P\), parallel to \(CA\) and \(CB\), intersect \(AB\) at \(D\) and \(E\) respectively. The line parallel to \(AB\) through \(P\) intersects \(CA\) and \(CB\) at \(F\) and \(G\) respectively. Show that the two lines \(DF\) and \(EG\) intersect on the circumcircle of the triangle \(ABC\).
