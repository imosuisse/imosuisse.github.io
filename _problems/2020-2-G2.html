Let \(ABC\) be a triangle with \(AB>AC\). The angle bisectors at \(B\) and \(C\) meet at point \(I\) inside the triangle \(ABC\). The circumcircle of the triangle \(BIC\) intersects \(AB\) again in \(X\) and \(AC\) again in \(Y\). Show that \(CX\) is parallel to \(BY\).
