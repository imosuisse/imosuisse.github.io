Let \(ABC\) be a triangle with \(AC > AB\). Let \(P\) be the intersection of \(BC\) and the tangent to the circumcircle of \(ABC\) through \(A\). Let \(Q\) be the point on line \(AC\) such that \(AQ = AB\) and \(A\) lays between \(C\) and \(Q\). Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be the midpoints of \(BQ\) and \(AP\), respectively. Let \(R\) be the point on \(AP\) such that \(AR=BP\) and \(R\) lays between \(A\) and \(P\). Show that \(BR = 2XY\).
