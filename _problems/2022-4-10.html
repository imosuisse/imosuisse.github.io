Let \(ABCD\) be a parallelogram such that \(AC=BC\). A point \(P\) is chosen on the extension of the segment \(AB\) beyond \(B\). The circumcircle of the triangle \(ACD\) meets the segment \(PD\) again at \(Q\), and the circumcircle of the triangle \(APQ\) meets the segment \(PC\) again at \(R\). Prove that the lines \(CD\), \(AQ\), and \(BR\) intersect at one point.
