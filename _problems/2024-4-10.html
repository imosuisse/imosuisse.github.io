Let \(ABC\) be a triangle with \(AC>BC\). Let \(\omega\) be the circumcircle of triangle \(ABC\) and let \(r\) be the radius of \(\omega\). Let point \(P\) lie on the segment \(AC\) such that \(BC=CP\) and let \(S\) be the foot of the perpendicular from \(P\) to line \(AB\). Let the line \(BP\) intersect \(\omega\) again at \(D \neq B\). Let \(Q\) lie on line \(SP\) such that \(PQ=r\) and such that \(S\), \(P\) and \(Q\) lie on the line in that order. Finally, let the line perpendicular to \(CQ\) from \(A\) intersect the line perpendicular to \(DQ\) from \(B\) at \(E\). Prove that \(E\) lies on \(\omega\).
