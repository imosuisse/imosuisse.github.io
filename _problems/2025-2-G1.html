Let \(ABC\) be a triangle with circumcircle \(\Omega\), and \(k\) be a circle passing through \(B\) and \(C\) such that \(A\) is in the interior of \(k\). The tangent to \(\Omega\) through \(A\) intersects \(k\) at two points \(P\) and \(Q\), such that \(P\) and \(C\) are on different sides of \(AB\). If \(M\) is the intersection of \(AB\) and \(PC\) and \(N\) is the intersection of \(AC\) and \(QB\), prove that \(MN\) is parallel to \(PQ\).
