--- year: 2018 round: 3 number: '10' ---

Let \(p\geq 2\) be a prime number. Arnaud and Louis alternatively choose an index \(i\in \{0,1,\ldots,p-1\}\) that has not already been chosen and a digit \(a_i\in\{0,1,\ldots,9\}\). Arnaud starts. Once every index has been chosen, they compute the following sum: \[a_0+a_1\cdot 10+\ldots+a_{p-1}\cdot 10^{p-1}=\sum_{i=0}^{p-1}a_i\cdot 10^i.\] If the sum is divisible by \(p\), Arnaud wins. Otherwise Louis wins. Prove that Arnaud has a winning strategy.
