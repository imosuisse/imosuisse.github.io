Let \(n\geq 3\) be an integer. Annalena has infinitely many cowbells in each of \(n\) different colours. Given an integer \(m \ge n+1\) and a group of \(m\) cows standing in a circle, she is tasked with tying one cowbell around the neck of every cow so that every group of \(n+1\) consecutive cows have cowbells of all the possible \(n\) colours. Prove that there are only finitely many values of \(m\) for which this is not possible and determine the largest such \(m\) in terms of \(n\).
