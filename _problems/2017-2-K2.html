--- year: 2017 round: Second Round number: 'C2' topic: Combinatorics ---

Let \(m, n \geq 2\) be positive integers. We have four colours and want to colour each unit square of a \(m \times n\) board with one of them such that in every \(2 \times 2\) square all four colours occur. How many different possibilities are there? Remark: We count two possibilities as different if there is at least one square which received different colours.
