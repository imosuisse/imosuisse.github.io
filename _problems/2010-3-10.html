--- year: 2010 round: 3 problem: 10 ---

Let \(n \geq 3\) and let \(P\) be a convex \(n\)-gon. Prove that \(P\) can be decomposed into triangles using \(n-3\) non-intersecting diagonals such that the circumcircle of each of these triangles contains all of \(P\). When exactly does such a decomposition exist?
