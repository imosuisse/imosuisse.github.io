Let \(a\), \(b\) and \(c\) be natural numbers. Determine the smallest value that the following expression can take: \[\frac{a}{\gcd(a+b,a-c)}+\frac{b}{\gcd(b+c,b-a)}+\frac{c}{\gcd(c+a,c-b)}.\]

Remark: \(\gcd(6,0)=6\) and \(\gcd(3,-6)=3\).
