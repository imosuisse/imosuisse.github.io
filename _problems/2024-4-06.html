Let \(n \geq 2\) be an integer. Kaloyan has a \(1 \times n^2\) strip of unit squares, where the \(i\)-th square is labelled with \(i\) for all \(1 \leq i \leq n^2\). He cuts the strip into several pieces, each piece consisting of a number of consecutive unit squares. He then places the pieces, without rotation or reflection, on an \(n \times n\) square such that the square is covered entirely and the unit square in the \(i\)-th row and \(j\)-th column contains a number congruent to \(i + j\) modulo \(n\). Determine the smallest number of pieces for which this is possible.
