Let \(a,d > 1\) be two coprime integers. Define the sequence \((x_i)_{i \in \mathbb{N}}\) by setting \(x_1 = 1\) and \[x_{k+1} = \begin{cases} x_k/a & \text{if } a \text{ divides } x_k\\ x_k + d & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}\] for all \(k\geq 1\). Determine the largest non-negative integer \(n\) such that \(a^n\) divides at least one term of the sequence, or prove that no such \(n\) exists.
