Let \(\pi=(a_1,a_2,\ldots,a_n)\) be a permutation of the numbers \(1,2,\ldots,n\). The scrambling of \(\pi\) is the number of pairs \((i,j)\) of positive integers with \(1 \leq i < j \leq n\) and \(a_j<a_i\). Prove that for every positive integer \(k\) with \(0 \leq k \leq \binom{n}{2}\) there exists a permutation of the numbers \(1,2,\ldots,n\) with scrambling \(k\).
