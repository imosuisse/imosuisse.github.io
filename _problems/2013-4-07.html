--- year: 2013 round: Selection number: 7 topic: Combinatorics ---

Let \(n \ge 2\) be a natural number. An \(n \times n\) board is completely covered with \(2\times 2\) squares. The \(2 \times 2\) squares consist of two diagonally opposite black unit squares and two diagonally opposite white unit squares. The \(2\times 2\) squares can be rotated and they can also overlap, but they must lie completely on the \(n\times n\) board. What is the minimum number of black squares visible in such a covering?
