For a finite set \(A\) of positive integers, we call a division into two disjoint non-empty subsets \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) demonic if the least common multiple of \(A_1\) is equal to the greatest common divisor of \(A_2\). Determine the minimum number of elements in \(A\) such that there are exactly \(2016\) demonic splits.
