Let \(k\) be a circle centred at \(O\) and let \(X,A,Y\) be three points on \(k\) in this order such that the tangent to the circumcircle of triangle \(OXA\) through \(X\) and the tangent to the circumcircle of \(OAY\) through \(Y\) are parallel. Show that \(\angle XAY = 120^\circ\) if \(A\) lies on the minor arc \(XY\).

Remark: The minor arc \(XY\) is the shorter arc of the circle \(k\) linking \(X\) and \(Y\).
