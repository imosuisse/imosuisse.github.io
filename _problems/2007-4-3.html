We call two people a friended pair if they know each other, and we call them a unfriended pair if they do not know each other (being friends or not being friends is always mutual). Let \(m,n\) be natural numbers. Find the smallest natural number \(k\) such that the following holds: In every group of \(k\) people, there are always \(2m\) people who form \(m\) disjoint friendly pairs, or there are \(2n\) people who form \(n\) disjoint non-friend pairs.
