Let \(\mathbb{P}\) be the set of all primes and let \(M\) be a subset of \(\mathbb{P}\), having at least three elements, and such that the following property is satisfied: For any positive integer \(k\) and for any subset \(A=\{p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_k\}\) of \(M\) with \(A\neq M\), all of the prime factors of the number \(p_1\cdot p_2 \cdot \ldots \cdot p_k - 1\) are contained in \(M\). Prove that \(M=\mathbb{P}\).
