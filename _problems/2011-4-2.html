The line \(g\) intersects the circle \(k\) at the points \(A\) and \(B\). The perpendicular bisector of the line \(AB\) intersects \(k\) again at \(C\) and \(D\). Now let \(P\) be another point on \(g\) that lies outside \(k\). The parallels to \(CA\) and \(CB\) through \(P\) intersect the lines \(CB\) and \(CA\) at the points \(X\) and \(Y\). Prove that \(XY\) is perpendicular to \(PD\).
