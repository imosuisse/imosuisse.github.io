Let \(ABC\) be a triangle with circumcircle \(\Gamma\). Let \(D \neq A\) be the second intersection of the internal bisector of \(\angle BAC\) with \(\Gamma\). We define \(E\) to be the intersection of line \(CD\) with the line perpendicular to \(BC\) through \(B\), and \(\omega\) to be the circumcircle of \(ADE\). The line parallel to \(AD\) passing through \(E\) intersects \(\omega\) at \(F \neq E\). Moreover, let the tangents to \(\Gamma\) at \(A\) and \(C\) intersect at \(T\). Prove that \(TF\) is tangent to \(\omega\).
