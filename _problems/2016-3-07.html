There are \(2n\) different points on a circle. The numbers \(1\) to \(2n\) are arbitrarily assigned to these points. Each point is connected to exactly one other point by a straight line, and none of these lines intersect. If a line connects the numbers \(a\) and \(b\), we assign the value \(|a-b|\) to the line. Show that we can choose the lines so that the sum of these values is \(n^2\).
