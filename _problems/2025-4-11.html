Let \(\Gamma\) be a fixed circle with two fixed points \(A, B\) on it. For a variable point \(P\not\in\{A, B\}\) on \(\Gamma\), let \(G\) be the center of gravity of the triangle \(ABP\). The parallel to line \(GP\) through \(B\) intersects \(AP\) at point \(C\), and the line \(CG\) intersects the circumcircle of the triangle \(ABG\) a second time at point \(Q\). Prove that as \(P\) varies, the point \(Q\) lies on a fixed circle.
