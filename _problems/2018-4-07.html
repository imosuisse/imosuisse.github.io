Let \(n\) be a positive integer. A sequence of \(3n\) letters is called Romanian if the letters \(I\), \(M\) and \(O\) appear exactly \(n\) times each. Define a swap to be the transposition of two adjacent letters. Prove that for any Romanian sequence \(X\), there exists a Romanian sequence \(Y\) such that \(Y\) cannot be obtained from \(X\) using fewer than \(\frac{3n^2}{2}\) swaps.
