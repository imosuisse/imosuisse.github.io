Consider an acute-angled triangle \(ABC\) where \(U\), \(V\), and \(W\) are the feet of the altitudes of \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) respectively, and where the orthocenter is denoted with \(H\). Let \(X\) lie on the segment \(AU\), \(Y\) on the segment \(BV\), and \(Z\) on the segment \(CW\). \(X,Y\) and \(Z\) are all different from \(H\). Show that \(X,Y,Z\) and \(H\) lie on a circle, if and only if \[[ABC] = [ABZ] + [AYC] + [XBC],\] where \([PQR]\) denotes the area of the triangle \(PQR\).
