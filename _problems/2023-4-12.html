For a positive integer \(m\), we denote by \([m]\) the set \(\{1, 2, \dots, m\}\). Let \(n\) be a positive integer and let \(\mathcal{S}\) be a non-empty collection of subsets of \([n]\). A function \(f\colon [n] \to [n+1]\) is called kawaii if there exists \(A \in \mathcal{S}\) such that for all \(B \in \mathcal{S}\) with \(A \neq B\) we have \[\sum_{a \in A} f(a) > \sum_{b \in B} f(b).\] Prove that there are always at least \(n^n\) kawaii functions, irrespective of \(\mathcal{S}\).
