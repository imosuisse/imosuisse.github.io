--- year: 2017 round: Selection number: 7 topic: Combinatorics ---

The Brazilian IMO leader chooses two positive integers \(n\) and \(k\) with \(n>k\) and shares them with their deputy and one of their contestants. The leader then whispers a binary sequence of length \(n\) to the deputy. The deputy then writes down all binary sequences of length \(n\) that differ from the shared sequence in exactly \(k\) position. The contestant then looks at all those sequences and tries to guess the sequence the leader initially chose. How many times does the contestant have to guess at least, until they are guaranteed to have been correct at least once.

Remark: A binary sequence is a sequence consisting of \(0\)s and \(1\)s. Example for \(n=3\) and \(k=1\): If the leader chooses \(101\), the deputy writes down \(001\), \(100\) and \(111\).
