Let \(ABC\) be a triangle with \(AB = AC\) and \(AB > BC\). Let \(k\) be the circle with center \(A\) through \(B\) and \(C\). Let \(H\) be the second intersection of \(k\) with the altitude of \(ABC\) though \(B\). Further, let \(G\) be the second intersection of \(k\) with the median of \(ABC\) through \(B\). Let \(X\) be the intersection of \(AC\) and \(GH\). Show that \(C\) is the midpoint of the segment \(AX\).
