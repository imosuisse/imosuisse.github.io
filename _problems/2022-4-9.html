Let \(ABCD\) be a quadrilateral inscribed in a circle \(\Omega\). Let the tangent to \(\Omega\) at \(D\) intersect the rays \(BA\) and \(BC\) at points \(E\) and \(F\), respectively. A point \(T\) is chosen inside the triangle \(ABC\) so that \(TE\) is parallel to \(CD\) and \(TF\) is parallel to \(AD\). Let \(K\neq D\) be a point on the segment \(DF\) such that \(TD=TK\). Prove that the lines \(AC\), \(DT\) and \(BK\) intersect at one point.
