For a positive integer \(n\) let \[f(n)=\frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^n \left\lfloor \frac{n}{k} \right\rfloor.\] Prove that there are infinitely many positive integers \(m\) for which the inequality \(f(m)<f(m+1)\) holds, and that there are infinitely many positive integers \(m\) for which the inequality \(f(m)>f(m+1)\) holds.
