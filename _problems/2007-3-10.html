A plane is divided into a grid of equilateral triangles of side length \(1\). Consider an equilateral triangle of side length \(n\), whose sides lie on the lines of the grid. On every lattice point on the edge and in the interior of this triangle lies a stone. In one turn, a unit triangle is selected which has a stone on exactly \(2\) corners. The two stones are removed and a new stone is placed on the third corner. For which \(n\) is it possible that only one piece remains after a finite number of moves?
