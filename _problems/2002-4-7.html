Let \(ABC\) be an equilateral triangle and \(P\) a point in its interior. \(X, Y\) and \(Z\) are the bases of the perpendiculars from \(P\) to the sides \(BC, CA\) and \(AB\) respectively. Show that the sum of the areas of the triangles \(BXP\), \(CYP\) and \(AZP\) does not depend on \(P\).
