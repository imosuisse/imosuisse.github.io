Let \(m,n\) be natural numbers. Consider a square grid of \((2m+1) \times (2n+1)\) points in the plane. A set of rectangles is called good if the following holds:

  • For each of the rectangles, the four vertices lie on grid points and the sides are parallel to the grid lines.

  • No two of the rectangles have a common corner point.

Determine the largest possible value of the sum of the areas of all rectangles in a good set.
