--- year: 2006 round: Second Round number: 5 topic: Geometry ---

An \(m \times n\)-board is divided into unit squares. An L-triomino consists of three unit squares: a central square and two outer squares. An L-triomino is located in the upper left corner of the board, the central square covering the corner square of the board. In a move one may rotate the triomino around the midpoint of one of its outer squares by multiples of \(90^\circ\). For which \(m\) and \(n\) is it possible to move the triomino to the lower right corner of the board using a finite number of moves?
