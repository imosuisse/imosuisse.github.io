Let \(x,y\) and \(a_0,a_1,a_2,\dots\) be integers satisfying \(a_0 = a_1 = 0\) and \[a_{n+2} = x\cdot a_{n+1} + y\cdot a_n + 1\] for all integers \(n\geq 0\). Let \(p\) be any prime number. Show that \(\gcd(a_p,a_{p+1})\) is either equal to \(1\) or greater than \(\sqrt{p}\).
