In the triangle \(ABC\) let \(\angle BAC = 60^\circ\). Let \(E\) be a point on the line \(AB\) such that \(B\) lies between \(A\) and \(E\) and \(BE = BC\). Similarly, let \(F\) be a point on \(AC\) such that \(C\) lies between \(A\) and \(F\) and \(CF = BC\). The circumcircle of \(ACE\) intersects \(EF\) in \(K\). Show that \(K\) lies on the angle bisector of \(\angle BAC\).
