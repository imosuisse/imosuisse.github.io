There are \(16\) secret agents working in Geneva. Each agent monitors at least one other agent, but no two agents monitor each other. Assume that for any \(10\) agents, they can be numbered in such a way that the first agent monitors the second, the second the third and so on, and the tenth the first. Show that for any \(11\) agents, they can also be numbered in such a way that each agent monitors the next.
