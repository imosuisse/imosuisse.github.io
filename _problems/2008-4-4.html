Two circles \(k_1\) and \(k_2\) intersect at \(A\) and \(B\). Let \(r\) be a line through \(B\) that intersects \(k_1\) at \(C\) and \(k_2\) at \(D\) such that B lies between \(C\) and \(D\). Let \(s\) be the line parallel to \(AD\) that touches \(k_1\) in \(E\) and that has the smallest possible distance to \(AD\). The line \(AE\) intersects \(k_2\) in \(F\). Let \(t\) be the tangent to \(k_2\) through \(F\). Prove that holds:

  1. The line \(t\) is parallel to \(AC\).

  2. The lines \(r\), \(s\) and \(t\) intersect at a single point.
