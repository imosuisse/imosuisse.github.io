Let \(ABC\) be an acute triangle with orthocenter \(H\), satisfying \(AC > AB > BC\). The perpendicular bisectors of \(AC\) and \(AB\) intersect line \(BC\) at \(R\) and \(S\) respectively. Let \(P\) and \(Q\) be points on lines \(AC\) and \(AB\) respectively, both different from \(A\), such that \(AB = BP\) and \(AC = CQ\). Prove that the distances from point \(H\) to lines \(SP\) and \(RQ\) are equal.
