Let \(ABC\) be a triangle and \(D\) a point in its interior. Let \(E\) be a point on the line \(AD\) different from \(D\). Let \(\omega_1\) and \(\omega_2\) be the circumcircles of the triangles \(BDE\) and \(CDE\) respectively. \(\omega_1\) and \(\omega_2\) intersect the segment \(BC\) at the points \(F\) and \(G\) respectively. Let the intersection of \(DG\) and \(AB\) be \(X\), and the intersection of \(DF\) and \(AC\) be \(Y\). Show that \(XY\) is parallel to \(BC\).
