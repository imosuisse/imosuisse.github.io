Let \(m\) be a positive integer greater than \(1\). The sequence \(x_0, x_1, x_2,\ldots\) is defined by \[x_i= \begin{cases} 2^i,&\text{for } 0 \leq i \leq m-1\\ \sum_{j=1}^{m}x_{i-j},& \text{for } i \geq m. \end{cases}\] Find the largest \(k\) such that there exist \(k\) consecutive elements in the sequence that are all divisible by \(m\).
