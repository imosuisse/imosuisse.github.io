--- year: 2012 round: Selection number: 5 topic: Combinatorics ---

Let \(n \geq 6\) be a natural number. Consider a set \(S\) of \(n\) distinct real numbers. Prove that there are at least \(n-1\) distinct two-element subsets of \(S\) such that the arithmetic mean of the two elements in each of these subsets is at least equal to the arithmetic mean of all elements in \(S\).
