Let \(p\) be a prime number and \(a\), \(b\), \(c\) and \(n\) positive integers with \(a, b, c<p\) such that the three assertions \[p^2 \mid a + (n-1)\cdot b, \qquad p^2 \mid b + (n-1)\cdot c, \qquad p^2 \mid c + (n-1)\cdot a\] hold. Show that \(n\) is not a prime number.
