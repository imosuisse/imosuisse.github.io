Consider a regular hexagon \(P\). For a given point \(A\), \(d_1 \leq d_2 \leq \cdots \leq d_6\) denote the distances between \(A\) and the six corners of \(P\), in order of their value. Find the locus of \(A\) inside and at the edge of \(P\), where

  1. \(d_3\) takes the smallest possible value.

  2. \(d_4\) takes the largest possible value.
