--- year: 2017 round: 3 number: '03' ---

The main building of ETH is a rectangle consisting of unit squares. Each side of a unit square is a wall, where some walls have doors. The perimeter walls of the building have no doors. Some number of Olympiad participants have gotten lost in the building. They can only move though doors between the unit squares. We assume that for any two unit squares, there is a way to get from one to the other.

Cyril wants to get all participants to assemble on the same unit square. To achieve this, he can broadcast the following instructions: North, South, East, and West. Each time he announces an instruction, all participants simultaneously try to move one unit square in the corresponding direction. If the wall in question has no door, they stay put.

Show that Cyril can achieve his goal by giving a finite number of instructions, no matter how the participants are initially distributed.
