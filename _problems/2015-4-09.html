--- year: 2015 round: Selection number: 9 topic: Combinatorics ---

Let \(n \geq 2\) be a natural number. There is a watchtower in the centre of a circular garden. At the edge of the garden are \(n\) evenly distributed garden gnomes. Attentive guards live on the watchtower. Each guard monitors an area of the garden that is bordered by two different garden gnomes. We say that guard \(A\) controls guard \(B\) if the entire area of \(B\) is included in that of \(A\). There are two groups of guards: Apprentices and Masters. Each apprentice is controlled by exactly one master and does not control anyone, while masters are not controlled by anyone. The whole garden has maintenance costs:

  • An apprentice costs \(1\) gold piece per year.

  • A master costs \(2\) gold pieces per year.

  • A garden gnome costs \(2\) gold pieces per year.

Show that the garden gnomes cost at least as much as the guards.
