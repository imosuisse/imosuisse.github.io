Consider a cube and two of its vertices \(A\) and \(B\), which are the endpoints of a diagonal. A path is a sequence of cube corners, with each step going from one corner along an edge of the cube to one of the three neighbouring corners. Let \(a\) be the number of paths of length \(2012\) that start in point \(A\) and end in \(A\) and let \(b\) be the number of paths of length \(2012\) that start in \(A\) and end in \(B\). Decide which of the two numbers \(a\) and \(b\) is the larger.
