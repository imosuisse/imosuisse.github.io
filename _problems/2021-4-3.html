Let \(p\) be an odd prime. Arnaud has hung up \(N\geq 1\) towels to dry on a washing line, each coloured either purple or yellow. Then, for each \(1 \leq n \leq N\), he calculates what fraction of the first \(n\) towels are yellow, and writes down these \(N\) fractions in their irreducible form on a piece of paper. Julia finds the piece of paper the next day and notices that all of the fractions \(\frac{1}{p}, \frac{2}{p}, \dots, \frac{p-1}{p}\) are on the paper. Prove that \[N \geq \frac{p^3-p}{4}.\]
