Let \(n \geq 4\) be an integer and let \(a_1, \ldots, a_n\) and \(b_1, \ldots, b_n\) be sequences of positive integers such that the \(n+1\) products \[\begin{aligned} a_1a_2 & \cdots a_{n-1}a_n, \\ b_1a_2 & \cdots a_{n-1}a_n, \\ b_1b_2 & \cdots a_{n-1}a_n, \\ & \; \; \vdots \\ b_1b_2 & \cdots b_{n-1}a_n, \\ b_1b_2 & \cdots b_{n-1}b_n, \end{aligned}\] taken in this order, form a strictly increasing arithmetic progression. Determine the smallest possible common difference of this arithmetic progression in terms of \(n\).

Remark: An arithmetic progression is a sequence of the form \(a, a+r, a+2r, \ldots, a+kr\) where \(a\), \(r\) and \(k\) are integers and \(r\) is called the common difference.
