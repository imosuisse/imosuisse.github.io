We call a positive integer likeable if the digits of its decimal representation fulfill the following two conditions:

  • Each of the digits \(0,1,\ldots, 9\) occurs at most once.

  • If \(A\) is an even digit and \(B\) is an odd digit, then there are exactly \(\frac{A+B-1}{2}\) other digits between \(A\) and \(B\).

Determine the number of likeable numbers.
