Let \(ABC\) be a triangle with \(\angle ABC \neq \angle BCA\). The incircle \(k\) of the triangle \(ABC\) touches the sides \(BC\), \(CA\) and \(AB\) at the points \(D\), \(E\) and \(F\) respectively. The line \(AD\) intersects \(k\) once more at \(P\). Let \(Q\) be the intersection of \(EF\) with the line perpendicular to \(AD\) through \(P\). Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be the intersections of \(AQ\) with \(DE\) and \(DF\) respectively. Show that \(A\) is the midpoint of the line \(XY\).
