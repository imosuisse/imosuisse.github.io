--- year: 2001 round: 4 problem: 5 ---

Let \(a_1<a_2< \ldots <a_n\) be a sequence of positive integers with the property that for \(i<j\) the decimal representation of \(a_j\) does not start with that of \(a_i\) (for example, the numbers \(137\) and \(13729\) cannot both occur in the sequence). Prove that \[\sum_{i=1}^n \frac{1}{a_i} \leq \frac{1}{1}+\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{3}+\ldots+\frac{1}{9}\]
