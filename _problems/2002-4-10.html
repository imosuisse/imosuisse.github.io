Let \(m\) be an arbitrary positive integer. As a function of \(m\), determine the smallest natural number \(k\) for which the following applies: If \(\{m, m+1, \ldots , k\} = A \cup B\) is any decomposition into two sets \(A\) and \(B\), then \(A\) or \(B\) contains three elements \(a, b, c\) (which do not necessarily have to be distinct) with \(a^{b} = c\).
