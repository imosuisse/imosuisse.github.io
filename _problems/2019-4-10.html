Let \(n \geq 5\) be an integer. A store sells juggling balls of \(n\) different colours. Each of \(n+1\) children buys three juggling balls of three different colours, but no two children buy exactly the same combination of colours. Show that there are at least two children who bought exactly one ball of the same colour.
