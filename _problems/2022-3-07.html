Let \(n > 6\) be a perfect number. Let \(p_1^{a_1}\cdot p_2^{a_2}\cdot \ldots\cdot p_k^{a_k}\) be the prime factorisation of \(n\) where we assume that \(p_1 < p_2<\ldots<p_k\) and \(a_i>0\) for all \(i=1,\ldots, k\). Prove that \(a_1\) is even.

Remark: An integer \(n\geq 2\) is called a perfect number if the sum of its positive divisors, excluding \(n\) itself, is equal to \(n\). For example, \(6\) is perfect, as its positive divisors are \(\{1, 2, 3, 6\}\) and \(1+2+3 = 6\).
