Let \(\Omega\) be a circle and \(\omega\) a circle inside \(\Omega\). Point \(A\) lies on \(\Omega\), and tangents to \(\omega\) through \(A\) touch \(\omega\) at \(B\) and \(C\). Let line \(BC\) intersect \(\Omega\) at points \(X\) and \(Y\). Let \(K, L\) and \(M\) be the midpoints of \(BC, AX\) and \(AY\) respectively. The circumcircle of \(XLK\) intersects \(\omega\) at two points \(P_1\) and \(P_2\), such that \(P_1\) lies on the same side of \(BC\) as \(A\). Similarly, the circumcircle of \(YMK\) intersects \(\omega\) at two points \(Q_1\) and \(Q_2\), such that \(Q_1\) lies on the same side of \(BC\) as \(A\). If lines \(P_1Q_1\) and \(P_2Q_2\) intersect at \(R\), prove that \(RA\) is tangent to \(\Omega\).
