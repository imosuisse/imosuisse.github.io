Let \(ABC\) be a triangle and \(D\) a point on the segment \(BC\). Let \(X\) be another point on the segment \(BC\) different from \(D\) and let \(Y\) be the intersection of \(AX\) with the circumcircle of \(ABC\). Let \(P\) be the second point of intersection of the circumcircles of \(ABC\) and \(DXY\). Prove that \(P\) is independent of the choice of \(X\).
