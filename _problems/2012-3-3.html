The circles \(k_1\) and \(k_2\) intersect at the points \(D\) and \(P\). The common tangent to the two circles on the side of \(D\) touches \(k_1\) in \(A\) and \(k_2\) in \(B\). The line \(AD\) intersects \(k_2\) a second time in \(C\). Let \(M\) be the midpoint of the chord \(BC\). Show that \(\angle DPM = \angle BDC\) holds.
