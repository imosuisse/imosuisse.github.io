Let \(n\) be a positive integer. Pingu the penguin and his \(n\) penguin friends are collecting salmon. Every penguin has at most \(n\) salmon, and no two penguins have the same number of salmon. In how many ways can the \(n+1\) penguins split into some number of groups of arbitrary sizes, such that each group has exactly \(n\) total salmon?
